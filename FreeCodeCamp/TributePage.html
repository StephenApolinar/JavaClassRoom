Dr. Jacques Denavit

Co-creator of Hartenberg-Denavit parameters, a present day mathematics convention describing robotic motion

Here's a timeline of Dr. Denavit's life:

  • 1930 - Born on October 1st in Paris, France.
  • 1952 - Denavit graduates from the University of Paris with undergraduate degrees in General Mathematics, and Physics.
  • 1953 - Denavit receives a Master of Science in Electrical Engineering from Northwestern University.
  • 1954 - Marries Catherine Dahlinger of Nashville, Tennessee.
  • 1955 - Co-authors A Kinematic notation for lower pair mechanisms based on matrices with Richard Hartenberg.
  • 1956 - Denavit continued advanced studies at Northwestern University, and earns a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering
  • 1958 - Accepts Professorship at Northwestern University in Mechanical, and Nuclear Engineering.
  • 1964 - Denavit co-authored Kinetic Synthesis of Linkages with Richard Hartenberg. The publication introduces Denavit-Hartenberg convention, also known as D-H convention. D-H convention is a mathematical frame of reference for the synthesis, and analysis, of spatial mechanisms.
  • 1969 - Accepts position as Research Physicist in the Plasma Physics Division of the Naval Research Laboratory until 1971.
  • 1981 - Richard Paul publishes Robot Manipulators demonstrating D-H conventions value for kinematic analysis of robotic systems.
  • 1982 - Leaves Northwestern University, and accepts position as Research Physicist at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.
  • 1993 - Retires from Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.
  • 2013 - Dies on September 12th in Pleasanton, Californa at the age of 82.
